Introduction: What is SIP?

A Systematic Investment Plan (SIP) allows you to invest a fixed amount at regular intervals (monthly/weekly) into mutual funds. SIP automates investing, builds discipline, and helps you benefit from compounding over time.

Why SIP is Important?

  • Affordability: Start with as low as â‚¹500 per month.
  • Rupee Cost Averaging: Buy more units when markets are low and fewer when high.
  • Compounding: Earnings generate further earnings over time.
  • Discipline: Automates long-term investing without timing the market.
  • Goal-based: Ideal for education, marriage, house, and retirement planning.

Types of SIPs

  • Regular SIP: Fixed amount at fixed frequency.
  • Top-up/Step-up SIP: Automatically increase SIP amount periodically.
  • Flexible SIP: Change SIP amount based on cash flow.
  • Perpetual SIP: No fixed end date; continue as long as you want.
  • Goal-based SIP: SIP designed to achieve a target corpus by a target date.

Best Practices

  • Link SIP to a clear financial goal and timeline.
  • Prefer equity funds for long-term goals (5+ years), debt/hybrid for short to medium term.
  • Increase your SIP annually (step-up) with income growth.
